Yab-Yum

10–20 min · chakra · breathwork · connection

1. Active partner sits cross-legged, tall, stable, and quiet. Take a few breaths alone first and become a still mountain of presence.
2. Receptive partner faces them, wrapping legs around the waist and arms over the shoulders. Let foreheads touch.
3. Let chests meet and pause for one full minute before breathing together.
4. Synchronize: inhale together for 4 counts, exhale together for 4 counts.
5. With each inhale, imagine light rising up the spine. With each exhale, let the heart soften and widen.
6. Continue for 10–20 minutes, ending in full silence.

Deep Reconnection

A tender heart-opening sequence after distance, conflict, or emotional fatigue.

Opening — 8 min: Lie side by side, fully clothed, one hand on your partner’s heart. Speak one true appreciation.
Attunement — 10 min: Move into Yab-Yum and synchronize breath completely. Release every agenda.
Flow — 15 min: Slow connection, no destination, no peak to chase.
Integration — 5 min: Hold each other with open eyes and one sentence of truth.

Jade Garden

10–20 min · yin-yang · chi circulation · harmony

1. Sit facing each other and establish a soft, even breath.
2. Begin slow circular pelvic movement, as if tracing a river, never rushing the body.
3. At high charge, pause and breathe upward instead of speeding forward.
4. Imagine energy rising up the spine and descending down the front body.
5. End before depletion. Tao prefers nourishment over collapse.

Back-to-Back Orbit

A restorative Tao practice for evenings when desire feels low but closeness is needed.

1. Sit or lie back to back.
2. Inhale from pelvic floor to crown; exhale down the front body.
3. Continue for 10 minutes until the nervous system feels steady, warm, and clear.

Still Union

A foundational Richardson practice.

1. Enter gently and simply stop moving.
2. Relax jaw, belly, and shoulders. Breathe softly through the nose.
3. Stay long enough for urgency to soften into subtle sensation.
4. End not with a rush but with gratitude and held stillness.

Front Rotation Flow

A slow sequence through several front-facing positions without breaking connection.

1. Begin seated face to face and establish breath synchrony.
2. Shift slowly through upright, reclining, and side positions, never hurrying transition.
3. If connection softens, stay where you are. Do not force movement.
4. Return to seated embrace and finish with a slow shared exhale.

Yab-Yum

10-20 minchakra · breathwork · connection

Yab-Yum ("father-mother" in Tibetan) is the foundational posture of Tantric sacred union. The receptive partner sits in the active partner's lap, face to face, legs wrapped around the waist. The practice is synchronized breathing in stillness — creating a shared energy circuit through all seven chakras. Called the "meditation of two" in Tibetan Vajrayana tradition.

  1. 1

    Active partner sits cross-legged, spine tall. Take 5 deep breaths alone first. Become a still mountain of presence.

  2. 2

    Receptive partner faces them, wrapping legs around the waist and arms over the shoulders. Let your foreheads touch.

  3. 3

    Let your chests touch. Feel the warmth radiating between your hearts. Stay here for a full minute before breathing together.

  4. 4

    Synchronize breath: inhale together for 4 counts, exhale together for 4 counts. Let nothing else exist.

  5. 5

    With each inhale, visualize white light rising from the base of your spine to the crown. With each exhale, expand from the heart.

  6. 6

    Continue for 10-20 minutes. The longer you stay, the deeper the merging. End with 5 minutes of complete silence.

💡

Play Tibetan singing bowl recordings. Do not use music with lyrics — the mind will follow words. Soundscapes allow presence.

Source: Tibetan Vajrayana tradition; Anodea Judith, "Wheels of Life"; Chogyam Trungpa, Tantric texts.

Shakti Wave

8-15 minfeminine energy · movement · polarity

This practice honors the feminine as the living creative force. The masculine partner becomes completely still, a grounded witnessing presence, while the feminine partner moves freely, trusting her body's wisdom. Stillness meeting aliveness, ground meeting wave. Drawn from SkyDancing Tantra (Margot Anand) and Neo-Tantric movement practices.

  1. 1

    Begin with 5 minutes of conscious breathing together. Arrive fully in your bodies before anything else.

  2. 2

    The masculine partner takes a grounded seated position. Close your eyes. Your only task is to be completely present and unmovable.

  3. 3

    The feminine partner begins to move. Start slowly — let the body lead. There is no choreography, only listening to sensation.

  4. 4

    Let breath and movement expand. Waves up the spine, arms rising, sounds emerging naturally. Expression, not performance.

  5. 5

    Masculine: witness with loving undivided attention. Do not guide, comment, or react. Your stillness is the gift.

  6. 6

    After 15-20 minutes, come together gently. Hold in stillness for at least 5 minutes. No words needed.

💡

"The feminine does not need permission to move. She needs a ground steady enough that she can trust her own trembling." — Margot Anand

Source: Margot Anand, "The Art of Sexual Ecstasy" (Tarcher, 1989); SkyDancing Tantra Institute lineage.

Firebird

8-12 minkundalini · breath · fire energy

Combines Kundalini Yoga's Breath of Fire with conscious lovemaking to activate Kundalini — the coiled energy at the base of the spine. Rather than suppressing this fire, the practice channels it upward through all seven chakras. Referenced in Kundalini Yoga tradition and Osho's Tantric meditations.

  1. 1

    Sit facing each other first. Do 3 minutes of Breath of Fire together: rapid, rhythmic, equal inhale-exhale through the nose.

  2. 2

    Move into connection slowly. Let the breath remain rhythmic and intentional throughout.

  3. 3

    Build movement gradually. Let breath lead movement. Energy follows breath.

  4. 4

    As intensity builds, consciously draw the energy upward through the body. A river of heat rising up the spine.

  5. 5

    Let authentic sound emerge — sighs, tones, laughter. Sound is the voice of energy moving.

  6. 6

    Slow gradually over 3-5 minutes. End lying face-to-face in complete integration.

Source: Osho's active meditations; Kundalini Yoga tradition as taught by Yogi Bhajan, 3HO Foundation.

Kneeling Mastery

8-15 min

A deeply connected kneeling position that aligns the spines of both partners, allowing kundalini energy to rise freely through shared breath. Both partners kneel, with the receptive partner in front receiving the full embrace and chest-to-back contact that unifies two heartbeats. Inspired by classical tantric asana practices.

  1. 1

    Both partners begin kneeling on a comfortable surface. Receptive partner moves in front, backs almost touching. Take three full breaths to arrive.

  2. 2

    Active partner wraps arms gently around the receptive partner from behind — one arm across the chest over the heart, one around the belly.

  3. 3

    Let your spines lengthen together. Feel the alignment from tailbone to crown. Breathe in unison — both inhaling and exhaling at exactly the same pace.

  4. 4

    Active partner: place your lips near the nape of their neck. Breathe warmth there. The neck is one of the most energetically sensitive channels in tantric anatomy.

  5. 5

    With each breath cycle, visualize energy rising together from the root upward — through the belly, heart, throat, and beyond. 8–12 breath cycles.

  6. 6

    Slow into stillness. Rest your foreheads together over one partner's shoulder. Complete the circuit in silence for 1–2 minutes.

Source: Tantric asana traditions; Margot Anand, The Art of Sexual Ecstasy; Neo-Tantra partner practices.

The Mermaid

8-15 min

A position of deep surrender and trust, where the receptive partner releases fully into the earth while the active partner provides complete support. The raised legs create an opening of the pelvis that Tantric practitioners describe as the flowering of the lower chakras. Named for the grace of resting half-suspended, half-held.

  1. 1

    Receptive partner lies on their back. Active partner kneels facing them. Begin with five breaths of eye contact before any movement.

  2. 2

    Receptive partner: slowly raise both legs, allowing them to rest on the active partner's shoulders or upper chest. Find a height that feels spacious, not strained.

  3. 3

    Active partner: place hands gently on receptive partner's hips — not gripping, but present. Feel the weight, the trust, the gift of being held.

  4. 4

    Receptive partner: let your lower back fully soften into the earth. Release all holding. The active partner is the support. You are the water.

  5. 5

    Both: breathe slowly and deeply. Each exhale, the receptive partner sinks a millimeter deeper into release. Each inhale, the active partner grows a millimeter steadier.

  6. 6

    After 8–12 minutes, lower legs slowly. Come together in side-by-side stillness for at least 3 minutes before speaking.

Source: Neo-Tantric somatic practices; Shakti Malan, The Art of Conscious Loving; embodied awareness traditions.

The Wall

5-12 min

Standing lovemaking carries a charge that horizontal practices rarely access — the full weight of presence, the pull of gravity, the meeting of two upright beings with nowhere to retreat. The wall creates a literal and metaphorical support — behind one partner, a boundary; in front, a conscious choice to remain.

  1. 1

    Stand facing each other. One partner finds the wall behind them — not leaning on it, but aware of it. Begin with forehead-to-forehead contact for one full minute.

  2. 2

    Active partner places forearms on the wall on either side of receptive partner's head — a frame, not a cage. This is pure masculine presence holding space.

  3. 3

    Eye contact throughout. If either partner looks away, pause and return. This practice lives or dies in the sustained gaze.

  4. 4

    Move slowly and consciously. Every movement is intentional. There is no speed, no rush — only the next present moment.

  5. 5

    Breathe together. Matched exhales release the effort. Matched inhales build shared charge. Let the breath be the metronome.

  6. 6

    When complete, remain standing together. Arms around each other. Let the wall hold you both for a final minute of stillness.

Source: Neo-Tantric embodiment practices; Urban Tantra by Barbara Carrellas; standing meditation traditions.

Sacred Arrival

The quality of how two people arrive together sets the entire tone of what follows. Most couples skip directly to action; this sequence teaches the art of the sacred threshold — the deliberate, conscious crossing from ordinary time into sacred time. Based on tantric ritual frameworks and the principle that preparation is itself the practice.

① Separation — 5 min

Begin apart. Each partner takes 5 minutes alone — shower, breathe, set a private intention. What do you wish to offer tonight? What do you wish to receive? Write one word on paper if helpful.

② Threshold — 5 min

Come together at the door to your space. Stand facing each other before entering. Make eye contact for one full minute without speaking. Only then, cross the threshold together, hand in hand.

③ Attunement — 10 min

Sit in Yab-Yum or back-to-back. Synchronize breath for a full 10 minutes. No agenda, no words. Just the sound of two people becoming one rhythm. This is the entire practice if that is all that happens.

④ Intention — 3 min

Each partner speaks their one-word intention aloud. Receive your partner's word as a gift. Then let all agenda dissolve. Whatever arises from this space is the sacred.

💡

The tantric tradition says: how you begin is how you will go. The Sacred Arrival sequence transforms not just intimacy but the entire quality of presence two people carry together.

Tantric Awakening

A complete arc designed to awaken and circulate energy through all three levels described in Tantric teaching: physical, emotional-mental, and spiritual. Each phase intentionally activates a different dimension. The sequence moves from body to heart to spirit and returns to integrated wholeness. Duration may vary — follow the energy, not the clock.

① Body Awakening — 8 min

Begin with Kneeling Mastery or back-to-back breathing. Focus entirely on physical sensation — warmth, pulse, breath, skin. No meaning-making yet. Pure body awareness for the first 8 minutes.

② Heart Opening — 10 min

Move into Yab-Yum. Place your right hand over your partner's heart. Speak one appreciation — specific and felt, not generic. Receive theirs. Then hold each other in complete silence as the words settle.

③ Energy Flow — 15 min

Let connection arise naturally from the heart space. Move into Firebird or The Mermaid — whatever the energy calls for. Maintain synchronized breathing throughout. This phase has no script, only presence.

④ Integration — 8 min

Come to complete stillness in any comfortable position together. Both close eyes. Feel the full circuit — body, heart, and something beyond both. Rest in what has been created between you.

💡

This sequence works because it honors the order: body first, then heart, then spirit. Trying to skip to the spiritual without passing through the physical and emotional is the most common tantric mistake.

Mohenjo-daro

20-30 minpelvis · life energy · muscle tone

Named after the ancient Indus Valley civilization whose art depicted sacred pelvic practices, Mohenjo-daro is a foundational tantric warm-up sequence that restores the natural flow of sexual energy. The practice works with breath and conscious pelvic muscle engagement to warm, awaken and strengthen the genital area — creating the energetic foundation for deeper tantric connection. Practiced in advanced tantric schools as the essential preparation before any sacred union.

  1. 1

    Sit facing each other cross-legged, spines tall, hands resting on your own lower belly just above the pelvis. Feel the warmth of your own hands.

  2. 2

    Begin slow pelvic floor contractions: inhale and gently squeeze the perineum upward, hold 3 seconds, exhale and release completely. Repeat 10 times.

  3. 3

    Extend the breath: inhale 4 counts drawing energy from the base of the spine upward, hold 4 counts feeling warmth expand in the pelvis, exhale 6 counts releasing any tension.

  4. 4

    Begin gentle, slow pelvic circles — one full rotation every 8 seconds. Imagine the pelvis as a bowl of warm golden liquid. Don't spill a drop.

  5. 5

    Synchronize your circles so you mirror each other. Eyes open, holding each other's gaze. Notice how the energy begins to build naturally.

  6. 6

    After 15 minutes, pause in stillness. Feel the warmth now present in your pelvis, belly, and lower back. This is the awakened foundation. Rest here together for 5 minutes before moving forward.

💡

Cold genital area is a sign of blocked energy, not low desire. This sequence, practiced regularly, restores natural flow and makes all other practices significantly deeper.

Source: Ancient Tantric tradition; referenced in Czech tantric school practices by leading instructors; foundational to Indus Valley sacred sexuality lineage.

Kayakalpa

25-35 minbreath · inner energy · rejuvenation

Kayakalpa means "body transformation" in Sanskrit — an ancient Siddha practice of cellular renewal through breath and energy circulation. In the tantric context, it is practiced as a deep energy restoration sequence, particularly powerful for women whose sexual energy flow has been depleted by stress, childbirth, or disconnection. The practice uses internal breath work and conscious energy visualization to warm, restore and establish the natural flow of kundalini — the life energy that rises through the spine.

  1. 1

    One partner (traditionally the feminine) lies in complete stillness, arms slightly open, eyes closed. The other partner sits at their crown, hands hovering a few centimeters above the head. Both breathe slowly.

  2. 2

    Lying partner: begin the internal breath of fire — rapid, rhythmic, equal inhale-exhale through the nose. 30 breaths. Feel the base of the spine begin to warm.

  3. 3

    Sitting partner: with each of your partner's exhales, visualize warm golden energy descending from your hands into their crown. You are a channel, not a source.

  4. 4

    Lying partner: slow the breath to deep, long waves. With each inhale draw warmth from the base of the spine upward through the belly, the heart, the throat. Feel each area warm and soften.

  5. 5

    Hold a complete breath at the crown for 5 seconds — feel the warmth there. Then exhale fully, releasing down through the entire body. Repeat 10 times.

  6. 6

    Both lie still in silence for 10 minutes. The renewed energy settles and integrates. Then switch roles if both wish to receive.

💡

This sequence is particularly recommended when one partner's sexual energy feels cold, low, or absent. It is medicine — not foreplay. Practice it separately from intimate connection.

Source: Siddha yoga tradition (Tamil Nadu, India); referenced in Czech tantric teaching lineages; practiced in advanced European tantra schools as a healing and restoration sequence.

10-20 minyin-yang · chi circulation · harmony

In classical Taoist texts, jade is the symbol of pure cultivated sexual essence. Sacred Path — Rules for Two

Ten invitations to walk together
  1. The phone is only the threshold. The real practice begins when you turn toward each other.
  2. Come to bed without armor. Just your body, your breath, and your willingness to be seen.
  3. Touch your partner as if your hands have never met their skin before — slowly, with no destination.
  4. What you withhold from each other, you withhold from yourself. Speak the tender thing.
  5. Disagreement is not the enemy of intimacy. Distance is. Come back, however small the step.
  6. The most sacred gift you can offer your partner is your undivided presence. Not advice. Not fixing. Presence.
  7. Let slowness be your teacher. The body opens only at its own sacred pace. Never rush the opening.
  8. Your partner is not your mirror — they are your mystery. Stay curious. Stay humble.
  9. Before any difficult conversation, breathe together first. Let the nervous system soften before the mouth opens.
  10. What cannot be said can often be held. Let silence be sacred between you. Not every feeling needs a word.
